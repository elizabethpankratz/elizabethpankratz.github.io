I'm a linguist based in Potsdam, Germany. My main interest is the interplay of language usage and cognition, and particularly the effects of this interplay on the morphology-syntax interface and on pragmatic reasoning. I'm also interested in NLP for morphologically complex languages.

Currently, I'm a researcher in the Collaborative Research Center 1412 in Project A04: Situated syntax at the Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin. I'm also finishing a Master of Science at the Universität Potsdam in Cognitive Systems: Language, Learning, and Reasoning under the supervision of Shravan Vasishth.

Before that, I did a Master of Arts in linguistics at the Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin. In my thesis supervised by Anke Lüdeling, I evaluated methods for quantifying changes in morphological productivity over time in diachronic corpora.
